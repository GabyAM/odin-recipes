Lasagna

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made with layers of flat, wide pasta sheets, typically layered with a rich, savory meat sauce, creamy béchamel sauce, and grated cheese, such as mozzarella and Parmesan.

These layers are stacked one on top of the other in a baking dish and then baked until the pasta is tender and the cheese on top is bubbly and golden brown. Lasagna is known for its hearty and comforting flavor, and it can be customized with various ingredients like vegetables, mushrooms, or even seafood to suit individual preferences. It is a popular and beloved pasta dish enjoyed in many parts of the world.

Ingredients

  • Ground beef or a mixture of beef and pork
  • Onion, finely chopped
  • Garlic, minced
  • Canned crushed tomatoes
  • Tomato paste
  • Olive oil
  • Red wine (optional)
  • Dried oregano
  • Dried basil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Sugar (to balance acidity, if needed)
  • Butter
  • All-purpose flour
  • Milk
  • Salt
  • Nutmeg (optional, for flavor)
  • Lasagna noodles (either pre-cooked or no-boil noodles)
  • Shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil or parsley for garnish (optional)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Meat Sauce
  2. Make the Béchamel Sauce
  3. Precook the Lasagna Noodles (if not using no-boil noodles)
  4. Assemble the Lasagna
  5. Bake
  6. Rest and Serve
